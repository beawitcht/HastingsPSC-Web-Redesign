STOP ARMS TO ISRAEL - SOLIDARITY WITH PALESTINE ACTION!
Thursday 3 July 3.30pm. General Dynamics, 100 Castleham Road, Hastings TN38 9NJ

While Israel is still enforcing its barbaric genocide on the people of Gaza, we will protest against the arms factory in our own town that profits from this unending evil (General Dynamics' profits were $3.7 billion last year).

We will stand with the fighters of Palestine Action, now under threat of proscription from the government. Palestine Action is one of the most effective groups campaigning against the genocide. The government has decided to ban it by listing it as a 'terrorist organisation.' This is outrageous. The government is supporting the terrorism that has been inflicted on the people of Gaza while banning an organisation that is fighting against that terrorism.

Facebook event page and Instagram page. Join us!

REPORT: EMERGENCY RALLY: FROM PALESTINE TO IRAN - STOP ISRAEL NOW

We called an emergency rally to condemn Israel's attacks across Iran, at the same time it had plunged Gaza into darkness by cutting off all internet access and enforcing a total siege across the West Bank. Israeli aggression knows no bounds - it has been given complete impunity by the West to destroy and kill without restraint. Our open mic rally had a range of great speeches including one by Irfan Chowdhury which covered the incredible history of the West's disgraceful interference in Iran. Thanks to everyone who came to support our rally, especially at short notice. It was really an inspirational event. Our report of the rally on Facebook, on Instagram and the speech by 12-year old Noah (Instagram).

A FILM BY LUJAIN TAMER MANSOUR: "BRING ME A THOUGHT I CAN ACT ON"
Saturday 5 July and Sunday 6 July, 11am - 3pm. Unit 2 8-10 London Road, St Leonards on Sea TN37 6AE

Unit 2 presents a new film from Palestinian artist Lujain Tamer Mansour. Having recently graduated from the Slade School of Fine Art, Tamer Mansour explores longing, memory and exile in this rich and powerful short film. Free entry - drop in. More information (website) and Instagram.
OH BIG BLUE - AN EXHIBITION OF POETRY AND ART BY PALESTINIAN CHILDREN
9 - 12 July 10am - 4pm, Stade Hall

Facebook event page.

Don't forget to book your place for the launch event at 6pm on Wednesday 9th July with renowned British poet Alice Oswald and an exclusive performance of a selection of the poems by local children. The event is free and open to all. Copies of the book will be available to buy on the night.

Written last year by children ages 9-17 from all over Palestine including Ramallah, Jenin, Hebron, Nablus, Jerusalem and Gaza, the collection of illustrated poems comes directly from the hearts and minds of Palestinian children growing up in a time of genocide and apartheid. They stand as a testament to courage, hope, resilience, and love.

Our upcoming exhibition is getting some great coverage in the press, including in Rye News, Sussex World and Skwawkbox. The free exhibition will be open daily 10am-4pm from 9th-12th July inclusive. To find out more.
THE ENCAMPMENTS: FILM SCREENING AND Q&A FUNDRAISER FOR AL MAWASI
Thursday 10 July 6:30pm, Stade Hall

Facebook event page. Ticket details.

Don't miss the chance to see the Hastings premiere of THE ENCAMPMENTS, the powerful new documentary following the Palestine solidarity protests that erupted across university campuses in 2024. Timely, urgent and filled with a clear-eyed fury, THE ENCAMPMENTS is a rallying cry for those who refuse to be silenced and a message of hope for the people of Palestine.

The screening will be followed by a discussion with UK student encampment organisers and is taking place in Stade Hall, where visitors can also view the Oh Big Blue exhibition of poetry and art by Palestinian children. It is a fundraiser for Al Mawasi with tickets priced Â£10/Â£8 concessions.
